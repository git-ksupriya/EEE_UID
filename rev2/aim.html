Reinforcement Learning-based Frequency Control for Renewable Energy Microgrids

This project focuses on using Reinforcement Learning (RL) to maintain stable frequency in a renewable energy microgrid by adjusting power flow intelligently.
We are also doing three phase stand-alone inverter design with a Droop and PI controller using MATLAB Simulink Main goals are

  • Stand-Alone Operation
  • Droop Control Implementation
  • Role of PI Controller
  • MATLAB Simulink Validation

1. Stand-Alone Operation : Stand-alone operation refers to a system that operates independently, without being connected to an external power grid or any other external power source.
(i) The inverter generates its own AC power from a DC source. It does not depend on the grid for power supply.
(ii) The system is completely disconnected from the main power grid.This is common in remote areas, off-grid locations, or during power outages.

2. Droop Control Implementation : Droop control is a decentralized control strategy used to regulate the frequency and voltage of an inverter's output in response to changes in load demand. It mimics the behavior of traditional power grids, where generators adjust their output based on load changes.
(i) In a stand-alone inverter system (or a microgrid), there is no central authority (like the main grid) to control the frequency and voltage. Droop control allows multiple inverters to work together seamlessly and share the load without needing communication between them.
(ii) Droop control works by adjusting the frequency and voltage of the inverter's output based on the active power (P) and reactive power (Q) demanded by the load. a.) 1. Active Power (P) vs. Frequency (f) Droop
When the load increases, the frequency tends to drop.
The droop control reduces the frequency slightly as the active power demand increases.
The relationship is given by : f=fo−mp⋅P
Where
f: Output frequency.
fo: No-load frequency (setpoint).
mp: Frequency droop coefficient.
P: Active power.

b.) Reactive Power (Q) vs. Voltage (V) Droop
When the load increases, the voltage tends to drop.The droop control reduces the voltage slightly as the reactive power demand increases. The relationship is given by :

V = Vo - nq . Q
Where
V: Output voltage.
Vo : No-load voltage (setpoint).
nq: Voltage droop coefficient.
Q: Reactive power.

3. Role of PI Controller : A PI controller is a feedback control mechanism that adjusts the output of a system based on the error (the difference between the desired setpoint and the actual output). It consists of two terms:

Proportional (P) Term: Responds to the current error.
Integral (I) Term: Responds to the accumulation of past errors.
The output of a PI controller is given by:
u(t) = Kp⋅e(t) + Ki⋅∫e(t) dt
Where:
u(t) : Controller output.
e(t) : Error (setpoint - actual value).
Kp : Proportional gain.
Ki​ : Integral gain.

What is a Microgrid:
A microgrid is a small power system that includes renewable energy sources (solar, wind), batteries, and loads. It can work independently or with the main power grid.

Problem:
Renewable energy is unpredictable, causing frequency variations that need to be controlled.

What is Reinforcement Learning (RL):
RL is a type of machine learning where an agent learns by interacting with the system and improving based on rewards.

RL Concepts in our Project:

State
The current condition of the microgrid, like frequency deviation and active power.
Action
The control actions taken, such as adjusting battery power or changing generator output based on state
Reward
A score given to the RL agent based on how well it maintains a stable frequency.

Goal of the Project:
The RL agent learns to take the best actions to keep the microgrid frequency stable, improving efficiency and reducing power fluctuations.
